Lunch Recommendations

Ten midday plates—noodles, rice bowls, and rolls—with clear steps so you can cook a satisfying lunch without guesswork.

Chicken teriyaki bowl

Sticky-sweet glaze with soy depth; savory chicken over mild rice with a glossy finish.

How to make it

  1. Pan-sear or bake chicken thighs until nearly cooked; slice if thick.
  2. Simmer soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and grated ginger until slightly thick.
  3. Glaze the chicken in the sauce until sticky and caramelized at the edges.
  4. Serve over rice with steamed broccoli and sesame seeds.

Quick beef pho

Aromatic broth with star anise warmth, lime brightness, and fresh herb lift.

How to make it

  1. Simmer beef stock with charred onion and ginger, cinnamon, star anise, and cloves for 30–45 minutes.
  2. Strain; season with fish sauce and a little sugar.
  3. Cook rice noodles; slice rare beef paper-thin (or use cooked brisket).
  4. Pour hot broth over noodles and beef; top with basil, mint, bean sprouts, lime, and chili.

Vegetable sushi rolls

Cool cucumber and creamy avocado with seasoned rice—clean, fresh, and lightly vinegary.

How to make it

  1. Season sushi rice with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt; cool to room temperature.
  2. Layer nori on a mat, spread rice, flip for inside-out rolls if desired.
  3. Fill with cucumber, avocado, carrot, and pickled radish.
  4. Roll tightly, slice with a wet knife, and serve with soy, wasabi, and pickled ginger.

Spicy tuna roll (cooked option)

Creamy-spicy filling with a kick of sriracha and mayo—rich and bold without raw fish if you use canned tuna.

How to make it

  1. Mix drained tuna with Kewpie mayo, sriracha, soy, and sesame oil.
  2. Prepare sushi rice and nori as for maki.
  3. Spread the spicy tuna along the rice, add cucumber for crunch, roll, and slice.
  4. Garnish with thin scallions and sesame seeds.

Pad Thai

Sweet, sour, salty, and nutty—tamarind tang with garlic chives and crunchy peanuts.

How to make it

  1. Soak flat rice noodles until pliable.
  2. Stir-fry tofu or shrimp with garlic; push aside and scramble egg.
  3. Add noodles and sauce (tamarind, fish sauce, palm sugar); toss until coated.
  4. Toss with bean sprouts and garlic chives; serve with lime, peanuts, and chili flakes.

Bibimbap bowl

Gochujang heat against mild rice and crisp vegetables—each bite is a new mix.

How to make it

  1. Prepare small portions of seasoned spinach, bean sprouts, carrots, and mushrooms.
  2. Add warm rice to a bowl; arrange toppings in sections.
  3. Top with a fried egg and a spoon of gochujang mixed with sesame oil and sugar.
  4. Stir everything together before eating.

Chicken katsu curry

Crisp panko crust with mild Japanese curry—sweet, spiced gravy over rice.

How to make it

  1. Bread chicken cutlets in flour, egg, and panko; shallow-fry until golden.
  2. Sauté onion and carrot; add water and Japanese curry roux blocks.
  3. Simmer until thick; adjust with honey if too sharp.
  4. Slice katsu, serve over rice, and ladle curry alongside.

Thai green curry (vegetables)

Coconut creaminess with green chile and basil—fragrant, spicy-sweet, and herb-forward.

How to make it

  1. Fry green curry paste in a little coconut cream until aromatic.
  2. Add remaining coconut milk, simmer with eggplant and bell pepper.
  3. Season with fish sauce, palm sugar, and lime leaves.
  4. Stir in Thai basil; serve over jasmine rice.

Shoyu ramen (weeknight)

Soy-forward broth with garlic and ginger—lighter than tonkotsu but still deeply savory.

How to make it

  1. Simmer chicken stock with garlic, ginger, dried shiitake, and soy sauce.
  2. Strain; season with mirin and a touch of sesame oil.
  3. Cook fresh ramen noodles; blanch bean sprouts and soft-boil eggs.
  4. Assemble bowls with noodles, broth, chashu or chicken, egg, nori, and scallions.

Banh mi–style sandwich

Crisp baguette, pickled crunch, cilantro coolness, and savory filling—bright and layered.

How to make it

  1. Quick-pickle carrot and daikon with vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  2. Grill or sear pork, tofu, or lemongrass chicken; slice thin.
  3. Split a crusty roll; spread mayo and Maggi or soy.
  4. Layer protein, pickles, cucumber, jalapeño, and plenty of cilantro.
