Dinner Recommendations

Ten evening mains—grills, braises, and stir-fries—each with tasting notes and step-by-step guidance.

Beef bulgogi

Sweet-savory soy and pear-tenderized beef with sesame and garlic—rich with a slight char.

How to make it

  1. Freeze ribeye slightly; slice paper-thin against the grain.
  2. Marinate for 30–60 minutes with soy, sugar, sesame oil, pear purée, garlic, and pepper.
  3. Stir-fry in a very hot pan in batches until edges caramelize.
  4. Serve with rice, lettuce wraps, and ssamjang if you like.

Teriyaki salmon

Buttery fish with lacquered glaze—sweet, salty, and a little smoky from the pan.

How to make it

  1. Pat salmon dry; season lightly with salt.
  2. Pan-sear skin-side down until crisp; flip briefly.
  3. Glaze with reduced soy, mirin, sake, and sugar until sticky.
  4. Rest a minute; serve with rice and quick-steamed greens.

Mapo tofu

Numbing pepper heat, fermented bean savor, and silky tofu—bold and deeply aromatic.

How to make it

  1. Stir-fry ground pork (optional) with doubanjiang until oil reddens.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and Sichuan pepper; bloom in oil.
  3. Gently simmer soft tofu in stock; thicken with cornstarch slurry.
  4. Finish with scallions and a drizzle of chili oil.

Korean lettuce wraps (ssam)

Smoky protein wrapped in cool lettuce with punchy sauce—interactive and fresh.

How to make it

  1. Grill or pan-cook marinated pork belly or beef strips.
  2. Wash butter lettuce or perilla leaves and dry well.
  3. Offer rice, ssamjang, kimchi, and sliced garlic on the side.
  4. Let everyone build bites: leaf, rice, meat, sauce, fold, eat.

Thai basil chicken (pad krapow)

Garlicky, chile-hot, and deeply savory with holy basil—best over rice with a crispy egg.

How to make it

  1. Pound garlic and chiles; stir-fry in oil until fragrant.
  2. Add ground chicken; break up and cook through.
  3. Season with soy, fish sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar.
  4. Fold in Thai basil off heat; serve with jasmine rice and fried egg.

Japanese curry rice

Mild, sweet curry with soft potatoes and carrots—comforting and family-friendly.

How to make it

  1. Sauté onion until soft; add chicken or beef and brown lightly.
  2. Add cubed potato and carrot; pour in water and simmer until tender.
  3. Off heat, stir in curry roux blocks until dissolved; simmer to thicken.
  4. Serve over short-grain rice with fukujinzuke pickles if available.

Kung pao chicken

Tangy vinegar-soy sauce, gentle heat from dried chiles, and crunchy peanuts.

How to make it

  1. Velvet chicken with cornstarch and a little oil; stir-fry until just cooked; remove.
  2. Fry dried chiles and Sichuan peppercorns briefly; add aromatics.
  3. Return chicken with sauce (soy, vinegar, sugar, stock, cornstarch).
  4. Toss with roasted peanuts and scallions; serve hot.

Steamed fish with ginger & scallion

Clean, delicate fish with aromatic steam—ginger warmth and soy-sesame finish.

How to make it

  1. Score a whole white fish; stuff and top with ginger and scallion whites.
  2. Steam 10–15 minutes depending on size until flaky at the thickest part.
  3. Heat oil until very hot; pour over fish with soy and a splash of water.
  4. Garnish with scallion greens and cilantro.

Simple home hot pot

Shared simmering broth—mushroom umami or mild chicken—whatever you dip picks up the flavor.

How to make it

  1. Simmer kombu dashi or chicken stock with garlic and ginger as the base.
  2. Arrange platters of thin meat, tofu, mushrooms, noodles, and greens.
  3. Keep broth at a gentle bubble at the table; cook items in batches.
  4. Dip in sesame sauce, ponzu, or chili oil as you like.

Dan dan noodles

Nutty sesame, spicy chile oil, and savory pork—slippery noodles with a slow-building heat.

How to make it

  1. Stir-fry minced pork with ya cai (pickled mustard) or substitute.
  2. Whisk sesame paste, soy, black vinegar, chili oil, and garlic for the sauce.
  3. Toss cooked wheat noodles with a little hot noodle water to loosen.
  4. Top with pork, sauce, scallions, and extra chili oil.
