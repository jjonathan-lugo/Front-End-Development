Dessert Recommendations

Ten sweet finales—mochi to mango rice—with flavor notes and steps that work in a home kitchen.

Mochi ice cream

Chewy, slightly sweet rice dough around cold ice cream—playful texture and clean sweetness.

How to make it

  1. Mix glutinous rice flour, sugar, and water; microwave or steam until a smooth, stretchy dough forms.
  2. Dust heavily with cornstarch or potato starch; knead briefly.
  3. Scoop small ice cream balls; freeze rock-solid on parchment.
  4. Wrap each in a thin layer of mochi; freeze again 20–30 minutes before serving.

No-bake matcha cheesecake

Earthy matcha against creamy cheese and a buttery base—slightly bitter and very smooth.

How to make it

  1. Press crushed cookies mixed with melted butter into a pan; chill.
  2. Beat cream cheese, sugar, and sifted matcha; fold in whipped cream.
  3. Pour over crust; smooth the top.
  4. Refrigerate several hours or overnight before slicing.

Red bean paste buns (simplified)

Fluffy bread with earthy-sweet adzuki filling—comforting and not overly sugary.

How to make it

  1. Use store-bought sweet red bean paste for the filling.
  2. Make a soft enriched dough; proof until doubled.
  3. Portion dough, wrap around paste balls, and pinch seams closed.
  4. Proof again; steam 12–15 minutes or bake until golden if you prefer baked buns.

Mango sticky rice

Coconut-creamy rice with ripe mango—tropical, floral, and rich without feeling heavy.

How to make it

  1. Soak glutinous rice; steam until tender.
  2. Heat coconut milk with sugar and salt; pour most over the rice and let absorb.
  3. Slice fresh mango alongside.
  4. Drizzle reserved thickened coconut sauce and sprinkle toasted mung beans or sesame if you like.

Dorayaki

Soft honeyed pancakes sandwiching sweet bean paste—nostalgic and snack-sized.

How to make it

  1. Whisk eggs, sugar, honey, water, and flour into a smooth batter; rest 15 minutes.
  2. Cook small rounds on low heat until bubbles form; flip once.
  3. Cool pairs of pancakes; spread red bean paste on one half.
  4. Sandwich and wrap edges gently.

Black sesame sweet soup

Toasty, nutty sesame with gentle sweetness—warming and aromatic.

How to make it

  1. Toast black sesame seeds until fragrant; cool.
  2. Blend with water and rice into a fine slurry.
  3. Simmer with rock sugar until thickened; stir often.
  4. Serve warm; optional glutinous rice balls on the side.

Taiyaki (filled pancakes)

Crisp outside, tender inside, with sweet filling—custard or red bean both work.

How to make it

  1. Mix pancake-style batter (flour, egg, milk, sugar, baking powder).
  2. Preheat a taiyaki iron; brush with oil.
  3. Fill molds partway, add filling, cover with more batter; close and cook until golden.
  4. Cool slightly before eating—filling is very hot.

Hong Kong egg tarts

Buttery flaky crust with silky, lightly sweet custard—delicate and rich in small bites.

How to make it

  1. Line tart molds with puff or shortcrust pastry; chill.
  2. Whisk eggs with hot sugar syrup and evaporated milk; strain twice for smoothness.
  3. Fill shells 80%; bake at moderate heat until custard just sets with a slight jiggle.
  4. Cool in the pan briefly before unmolding.

Shaved ice halo-halo style

Cold, creamy, and playful—jackfruit, beans, jellies, and ube in every spoonful.

How to make it

  1. Layer sweet beans, coconut jellies, jackfruit, and kaong in a tall glass.
  2. Add shaved ice; pour evaporated milk.
  3. Top with leche flan chunk and ube halaya.
  4. Stir well before eating to mix textures.

Green tea affogato

Bitter matcha meets vanilla ice cream—hot meets cold in one simple dessert.

How to make it

  1. Whisk high-quality matcha with hot water until frothy and smooth.
  2. Scoop vanilla or coconut ice cream into small bowls or cups.
  3. Pour matcha over the ice cream at the table.
  4. Eat immediately while textures contrast.
