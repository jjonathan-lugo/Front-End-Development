Why cook Asian at home?

If you are looking for bold flavors and culture in your day this is the right place for you. Asian food is nourishing, offers comfort, and can satisfy your taste buds. Many of the foods made are easier than you expect them to be to make. Cooking these meals will save you a lot of money and allow you to customize flavors to your liking giving you something new to experience. What are you waiting form turn your kitchen into a place where you can explore and become creative with every meal.

Kitchen tips

Click a question to expand for suggestions when cooking.

Toast aromatics, add a spoon of miso or soy, and finish with a splash of rice vinegar or lime. A short fry builds serious flavor.

A good toasted sesame oil: use it at the end of stir-fries and soups so the aroma stays bright.

Rinse if the recipe says so, toss with a tiny bit of oil if serving cold, and add to broth at the last second when eating hot.

Top Picks

  • Pho or ramen — Build flavor in the broth; toppings make it yours.
  • Homemade dumplings — Freeze extras; they cook straight from frozen.
  • Kimchi fried rice — Use leftover rice and jarred kimchi for a ten-minute win.
  • Miso soup — Warm, savory, and ready in minutes beside any main.
